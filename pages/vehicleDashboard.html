VEHICLE SALES INSIGHT DASHBOARD

Interactive Looker Studio dashboard for vehicle sales insights with regional maps, trend analysis, customer rankings, revenue shares, deal size distributions, and KPI-driven performance evaluation.

View on Looker Studio

Project Overview

This dashboard is built from a vehicle sales dataset sourced from Kaggle, which I cleaned and prepared in Python before analysis. The raw dataset contained inconsistent formatting, missing values, extra columns, and encoding issues.

After cleaning, the dataset contains 1,518 sales transactions across multiple regions and product lines, with details like units sold, selling price, sales revenue, customer info, product category, geographic location, and deal size.

The purpose of this dashboard is to provide an interactive and visual exploration of sales performance, helping business stakeholders identify trends, top customers, and revenue distribution patterns with confidence in the data’s reliability.

Dashboard Walkthrough

Let's explore each component of this comprehensive sales dashboard and understand how each visualization contributes to actionable business insights.

Key Performance Indicators

The scorecards provide quick, high-level KPIs that give stakeholders an immediate understanding of overall performance:

Total Units Sold – The sum of all vehicle units sold across the dataset, reflecting overall sales volume.
Average Selling Price – The mean selling price across all transactions, helping gauge general pricing levels.
Total Yearly Sales – Aggregated yearly revenue for tracking annual performance and growth.
Customers – Total unique customers served, providing insight into market reach and customer base size.

Interactive Visualizations

Global Sales by Region

A geographic map showing total sales revenue by country or region. This chart quickly highlights where the largest revenue streams are coming from, allowing businesses to identify high-performing markets and potential expansion opportunities.

Monthly Sales Trend

A line chart tracking monthly sales from January to December. This reveals seasonal patterns, sales peaks, and slow months, helping plan marketing campaigns and inventory allocation.

Top 10 Customers by Revenue

A ranked bar chart showing the top 10 customers based on total revenue. This helps identify key accounts that drive a large share of sales, which can be targeted for loyalty programs or premium offers.

Product Line Sales Share (%)

Displays the percentage contribution of each product line to total sales. This is useful for understanding which product categories dominate the market and where diversification or promotion might be needed.

MSRP vs Actual Selling Price

Plots the manufacturer's suggested retail price (MSRP) against the actual selling price. This allows for spotting discounting trends, negotiation ranges, and possible underpricing issues.

Sales Revenue by Deal Size

Shows how total revenue is distributed across deal sizes (Small, Medium, Large). This helps understand whether most income comes from big-ticket deals or higher-frequency smaller sales.

Number of Deals by Deal Size

Counts the number of transactions per deal size category. This complements the previous chart by showing volume-based distribution, revealing whether high-value deals are common or rare.

This dashboard not only delivers clear business insights but also presents the data in an engaging, intuitive format—perfect for decision-making and portfolio demonstration.
